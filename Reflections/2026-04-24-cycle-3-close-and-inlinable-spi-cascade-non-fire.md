## Patterns and Root Causes

**Shape-vs-Decisions latent-contradiction pattern.** Doc 1 was structured as *investigation body* (Option 5 Shape as prose) + *Principal Decisions table* (answers to 6 escalated open questions). The Shape said "delegate to `POSIX.Kernel.IO.Write.write` (the already-retry-wrapped L3 raw)"; Decision #2 said "method surfaces Interrupt for caller; free-function retries internally — pick your abstraction." These are structurally contradictory: one specifies delegating to the form that SWALLOWS EINTR; the other promises to PROPAGATE EINTR.

Why did this slip past investigation close? The six escalated open questions were about the ERROR SHAPE (the domain Decision #2 covers), not about the DELEGATE TARGET. The delegate target was buried in Shape prose and never distinctly surfaced as its own question. When Principal answered #2 in favor of EINTR visibility, the *implicit* resolution was "this also overrides Shape's retry-wrapped delegate" — but the override was never made explicit. Future investigations should treat Shape + Decisions as a *coherence contract*: any Shape clause contradicted by a Decision's runtime claim MUST be amended or cross-referenced inline, not left to implicit re-interpretation at implementation time.

This is a sibling of [HANDOFF-016]'s proposal/premise-staleness axes — except the failure mode is *intra-document self-contradiction* rather than *cross-session premise drift*. Both occur because authoring-time coherence isn't re-verified at read-time. A mechanical post-authoring sweep ("for each Decision making a runtime claim, grep the Shape section for contradictory clauses") would have caught this cheaply at Doc 1's authoring.

**@inlinable + @_spi transitive-reach semantic correction.** Doc 1's Option 5 amended Cons paragraph predicted that `@inlinable`-via-transitive-reach-to-`descriptor._rawValue`-which-is-`@_spi(Syscall)` would fire the `@_spi`-in-`@inlinable` compile error. The prediction was rooted in the 2026-04-20 `l2-l3-same-signature-latent-ambiguity` reflection (which itself addressed same-signature ambiguity; the cascade prediction was inferential extension, not empirical finding).

Empirically, via the rule-#2(a) pre-check, the cascade does NOT fire. Swift's `@_spi`-in-`@inlinable` rule is a **direct-reference** rule — it checks whether the `@inlinable` function's body text directly references an `@_spi` symbol. A call through a non-`@inlinable` public callee severs the transitivity: `ISO_9945.Kernel.IO.Write.write` is a public non-`@inlinable` function; its own body can reference `@_spi` symbols freely (because it isn't `@inlinable`); an `@inlinable` caller can invoke that public entry point without inheriting the `@_spi` constraint.

Load-bearing ecosystem-wide: it means L3 extensions on L1 types (the Option-5 method-level split pattern) CAN be `@inlinable` as long as they compose through public non-`@inlinable` entry points — the normal L3 composition shape. Future P2.2-class investigations should treat the `@inlinable`+`@_spi` cascade as a *direct-reference* risk in function-body prose, not a transitive risk through the call graph. The 2026-04-20 parent reflection might benefit from a follow-up clarifying that its original observation was same-signature-specific and the `@_spi` cascade inference was narrower than the rule actually implements.

**Cross-cycle latent ambiguity from cycle-local gate scopes.** Cycle 2's `Kernel.Signal.Information` landing at `2a5e5fe` combined with swift-linux-standard's prior `fd04244` declaration to create a Linux-only latent ambiguity. Cycle 2's Docker gate on swift-iso-9945 passed clean (10.60s at Cycle 2 close) because its scope was narrow — iso-9945 in isolation doesn't exercise the swift-linux-standard + swift-kernel-Linux consumer configuration where both `Kernel.Signal.Information` declarations become visible. Cycle 3's broader gate (d) on swift-kernel "Kernel File" target on Linux was the first verification to exercise that configuration and thus the first to surface the ambiguity.

The pattern: cycle-local gates are scoped to each cycle's diff. Cross-cycle ecosystem effects from parallel-cycle work only surface when a LATER cycle's gate happens to cover the broader consumer surface. Mitigation options differ in cost/coverage:

- **Broader per-cycle gates** — expensive; each cycle pays full-ecosystem verification cost.
- **Arc-close cross-cycle gate** — cheaper amortized; one full verification at the end of a multi-cycle arc.
- **Post-arc `/audit` pass** — what the user scheduled here; runs after `/reflect-session` and before the next cycle's prioritization; naturally surfaces the Signal.Information ambiguity as its own finding.

The user's scheduled post-Cycle-3 `/audit` is structurally the right pattern: cross-cycle verification amortized at the arc boundary, where prioritization is already happening and the cost is justifiable.
