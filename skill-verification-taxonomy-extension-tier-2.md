## Context

### Trigger

Extension of the three-class verification taxonomy (`mechanical / hybrid / semantic`) from the 13-skill tier-1 dispatch (`skill-verification-taxonomy-extension-tier-1.md` v1.0.0, 236 requirements) to the **7 tier-2 code-shape skills** — the cluster governing how Swift code is structured at the type / ownership / module level. The handoff brief (`/Users/coen/Developer/HANDOFF-classification-extension-tier-2.md`) predicted moderate mechanical-rule density, between tier-1's 57% and tier-3's expected lower rate, with strong AST-checkable predicates on type-shape rules and more semantic content on ownership/layering judgment calls. Dispatched as **dispatch 2 of 3** in the tiered classification sweep; tier-3 (voice/process skills, ~24 skills) is the final dispatch gated on this one's review.

## Question

The pilot's three coupled questions restated at tier-2 scale:

1. **Coverage**: When applied across 7 tier-2 code-shape skills (208 requirements), does the three-class taxonomy classify cleanly, or does it surface a resistant set materially larger or differently shaped than tier-1's 19.9%?
2. **γ-roadmap saturation**: How densely do tier-2 mechanical rules map to already-designed γ-roadmap CI checks? (Brief predicted sparse — the centralized-CI doc's γ family is workflow/metadata-shaped, not code-shaped.)
3. **Rollout-relevance**: Does the tier-2 distribution surface anything that would change the pilot+tier-1's recommendation to adopt the taxonomy with three remediation clarifications + three new annotations, or does it stress-test the recommendation's robustness?
